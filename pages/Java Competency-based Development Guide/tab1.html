Proficiency Level Definition

Basic training has been received. The only experience gained has been in classroom and/or experimental scenarios or as a trainee on-the-job. You would be expected to need some help when performing the skill.

Repeated successful experiences have been completed. Help from an expert may be required from time to time, but you can usually perform the skill independently. You typically have 3-5 years of experience with the skill.

You can perform the actions associated with this skill without assistance. You are certainly recognized within your immediate organization as "the person to ask" when difficult questions arise regarding this skill. You are probably also known outside your organization as an expert. You have extensive experience and could teach the subject if you had teaching skills. You typically have 5-7 years experience with the skill, but may have less.

You can answer any questions about the skill and most any question related to the field where the skill is used. You have probably published a paper on the subject. The "expert" would come to you for advice. You typically have 10 or more years of experience with the skill, but may have less.
